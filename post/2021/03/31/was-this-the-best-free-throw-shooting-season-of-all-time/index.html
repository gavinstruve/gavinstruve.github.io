Was this the best free throw shooting season of all time?

2021-03-31

Oral Roberts missed out on making history on March 27 in its loss to Arkansas. And no I’m not talking about becoming the first 15 seed to reach the Elite 8. The Golden Eagles’ Cinderella run to the Sweet 16 was nothing short of spellbinding, but in the process, they missed out on toppling a 37-year old record. I’m talking about Harvard’s team free throw percentage record. During the 1983-84 season, the Crimson went 15-11…and shot 82.2% from the free throw line as a team, a mark that has stood for nearly four decades.

Now, in the strangest season, Oral Roberts, a tiny evangelical school, was on the verge of breaking it. Had junior forward Francis Lacis made the front end of his pair of free throws in the final minute of the 2-point loss to Arkansas, Oral Roberts would have made 408 of its 496 free throws on the season, good for 82.3% and a new NCAA record. Instead, the 407 makes left the Golden Eagles at 82.1% on the season.

In all, five teams shot above 80% and 20 teams shot at least 78%. In the 2019-20 season, no team topped 80%, and in 2018-19, two teams did but only six total shot hit 78%, per sports reference. This begs the question, is this the most historic free throw shooting season in college basketball despite no team breaking Harvard’s record?
